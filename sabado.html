Sábado

Casual beachwear ecommerce website for modern men who like spending good time at the beach. Made in Portugal.

Design process

Approach

This project started by a personal need for shopping beachwear. I realised there were few ecommerce websites in Portugal only focusing on men, and none providing all that’s necessary to enjoy the beach. I confirmed this problem by conducting a market study. From there, I defined the brand values and how they would be communicated. I then jumped on the conception of the website by delivering the following: user stories, user flow diagram, paper sketches, and high fidelity interfaces. From there I was able to test a mobile version with potential customers and iterate accordingly. To finish, I designed the responsive tablet and desktop versions.

Branding guidelines

I decided early in the process what the brand would stand for and how it would be communicated. From a set of values, I defined a dedicated name, logo, color palette, tone of voice, as well as a typography. All such elements of Sábado are complementary and all together convey a unique brand identity.

User flow diagram

The user flow diagram of Sábado was created based on user stories. Even though the overall structure is similar to most ecommerce websites, I wanted to differentiate Sábado from other websites. Therefore, I decided to focus my attention on the core experience to create a memorable buying journey.

Sketching & Wireframing

With pen & paper, I started sketching my ideas. I then turned such low fidelity drawing into digital wireframes. Of course, in this process, I iterated quite a lot and therefore improved step by step the overall experience.

High fidelity screens

I applied the initial branding guidelines to the digital wireframes to get to high fidelity interfaces. In this step, I also carefully curated a set of photos that carry the brand emotions.

Prototype & Testing

I assembled all the screens together in order to see how the various screens would work together. This prototype brought the website to life, which allowed me to test it with potential buyers. The feedback was positive, and confirmed I have delivered a solid beachwear ecommerce experience.

Responsive versions

As I got to a strong mobile version of the website, I could take it from there and develop its responsive versions for tablet and desktop.

Conclusion

Sábado received good feedback from potential buyers, and that’s what was the most important to me! if users would be ready to buy their beachwear on this website, it means the goal of the project was achieved. From a personal perspective, I was happy with the results and the skills I have developed related to ecommerce and responsive design.
